PHYSICAL LAYER

1. Purpose of the Physical Layer

       a. Physical Connection - NICs connect devices to networks via wired or wireless methods.
       b. Physical Layer Functions - Transport bits as signals.
                                   - Encodes and decodes data frames.
2. Physical Layer Characteristics

       2.a Physical Components - include hardware like NICs, interfaces, cables, and connectors.
       2.b Encoding - Converts bits into recognizable patterns.
       2.c Signaling - Methods vary: electrical (copper), Light (fiber-optic), microwave (wireless)
       2.d Bandwidth - Measures data transfer capacity
             Terminologies:
             Latency: Time for data to travel.
             Throughput: Acutal transfer rate.
             Goodput: Usable data rate.

3. Copper Cabling

       a. Characteristics - Common, affordable, east to install.
                         - Susceptible to attenuation and interference.
       b. Types - UTP, STP, Coaxial.
       c. UTP (Unsheilded Twisted Pairs) - Relies on cancellation and twist variations.

4.Fiber-Optic Cabling 

      a. Long distance, high bandwidth, immunte to EMI/RFI.
      b. Types: Single-mode, Multimode
      c. Usage: backbone cabling, FTTH, Submarince networks.
      d. Connectors: Types: ST, SC, LC
      e. Patch Cords: Color-coded for mode type.

  Resources: https://www.globalspec.com/learnmore/optics_optical_components/fiber_optics/fiber_optic_cable

5. Wireless Media

       a. Properties: Mobility, electromagnetic signals. Limitations: Coverage, interference and security.
       b. Standards: Wi-Fi (802.11), Bluetooth (802.15), WiMax (802.16), Zigbee (802.15.4)
